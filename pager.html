Introduction

Before smartphones and instant messaging, the Pager (also called a beeper) was the ultimate symbol of connection. From doctors to business professionals, this tiny device carried messages faster than letters and cooler than a phone call.

Origin Story

The first pager system was introduced by Motorola in 1950, originally designed for hospitals.

By the 1980s and 1990s, pagers had become a global communication trend allowing people to receive short numeric or text messages almost anywhere.

How It Worked

A pager used radio signals to receive messages sent from a central paging network.

When a message arrived, it displayed a beep, vibration, or flashing light, alerting the user to check the screen.
