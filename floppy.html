Introduction

Before USB drives and cloud storage, the Floppy Disk was the heartbeat of personal computing. From saving assignments to transferring games between friends, this small magnetic square carried the weight of digital history.

Origin Story

Invented by IBM in 1967, the floppy disk revolutionized how people stored and shared data. Early versions were 8 inches wide, followed by the popular 5.25-inch and 3.5-inch models that dominated the 1980s and 1990s.
